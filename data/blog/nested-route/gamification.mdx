The use of game-like components and ideas in non-game environments to engage and inspire individuals to complete certain goals or tasks is known as gamification. These ideas are used in the context of community gamification to promote participation, collaboration, and interaction within a community. The goal is to improve the community experience so that members will be more engaged and loyal.

In this article we aim to explore the key elements and components that contribute to creating a successful game-like experience for your community members. We will also look at how you can foster healthy competition, emphasizing the significance of incorporating an element of mystery in the rewards system, and maintaining a balance between simplicity and engagement.

### Gamification Elements

Elements of gamification refer to the fundamental building blocks and features used to create engaging and motivational experiences. These elements are inspired by game design principles and are integrated into non-game contexts to encourage specific behaviors and achieve desired outcomes. Some common gamification elements include:

<ol>
  <li>
    <strong>Scores and Points:</strong> The first element of Gamification that we are going to talk
    about is scores and points. Here we allocate points to users upon them competing a certain task
    in the community, this will boost the motivation and enhance their loyalty towards your product
    or service. The activities may range from Points for them updating their profiles completely,
    Points for posting initiating discussion in the community, points for answering questions in the
    community, etc. Points can be categorised as Experience Points, Reputation Points, Redeemable
    Points, Skill Points, Karma Points.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Badges:</strong> As described above, Badges are crucial in gamification because they
    make the user feel important in your community and skilled. Badges may range from virtual
    ribbons, stickers, virtual designs from your team based on the companies theme, or prizes that
    community members can earn from engaging in a certain task in the community or compeleting a
    certain activity in the community.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Leaderboard:</strong> A leaderboard is a list that ranks users based on their
    performance in a game, competition, or other activity. Leaderboards are often used to track
    progress, motivate users, and create a sense of competition within the community.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Challenges and Quests:</strong> Challenges and quests are thoughtfully designed and
    structured tasks or missions that participants can willingly undertake within a gamified
    experience. These engaging endeavors serve multiple purposes, including enabling participants to
    showcase their abilities and skills while striving to achieve specific objectives. As
    participants progress through these challenges, they earn valuable rewards, recognition, or
    points, providing a tangible sense of accomplishment and incentivizing continued engagement.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Logos and Avatars:</strong> Logos and avatars are customizable digital representations
    for participants in the community that can reflect their progress, achievements and their trust
    level in the communnity.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Progress Bar:</strong>Progress bars are a common gamification element that is used to
    track progress and motivate users. They are often used in conjunction with other gamification
    elements, such as points, levels, and leaderboards.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Give Aways and Rewards:</strong> Giveaways and rewards are a famous gamification system
    that can be used to reward users for completing tasks in the community, the tasks can range from
    signing up for a newsletter, making a purchase, or referring a friend, answering questions in
    the community etc. This is used to encourage users to participate in the community and create a
    sense of community to users.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Other Gamification Programs:</strong> <strong>Community Recognition,</strong>{' '}
    <strong>Trust Levels,</strong> <strong>Rewards,</strong> <strong>Unloackable Content</strong>{' '}
    (Access to additional features, content, or privileges that become available as participants
    reach specific milestones), <strong>Social Media Shoutouts</strong>,{' '}
    <strong>Social Media Treasure Hunt</strong>,{' '}
    <strong>Progress Bar for filling out profile</strong>, <strong>Twitter Polls</strong>.
  </li>
</ol>

### Examples of Gamification Techniques that are working currently

#### TikTok Gamification

TikTok is a social media platform that uses gamification to keep users engaged. Some of the ways that TikTok uses gamification include:

<ol>
  <li>
    <strong>Points:</strong> Users using the TikTok platform earn points for completing certain
    tasks, such as watching videos, liking videos, and following other users. These points can be
    redeemed for rewards, such as virtual gifts or access to exclusive content.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Challenge/Contest</strong> In this gamified experience, users engage in an interactive
    game known as "Match." Within this game, participants receive rewards while interacting with
    content or playing specific game elements. The rewards earned by users are carefully tracked and
    displayed on an intuitive progress bar, allowing each participant to monitor their advancement
    throughout the contest.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Verification Badges:</strong> TikTok awards users with verification badges upon users
    confirming that the account belongs to the person or brand it represents
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>TikTok Gifts:</strong> TikTok has its own donation system which allows users to send
    their favourite creators real money in the form of gifts which can be bought with TikTok coins.
    However, the creators will have to be on a Live in order for people to send them gifts. Each
    virtual gift costs a different amount of coins, the gifts range from Rose, Lion, Universe,
    Whale, Donut etc, each gift has its own value tied it.
  </li>
</ol>
