### Overview of NFTs and Blockchain Technology

### NFTs: Definition and Key Elements

There is this myth that goes that NFTs are just over valued JPEGs in real sense when we looking at NFTs we focus on:

<ol>
  <li>The Asset itself,</li>
  <li>Ownership,</li>
  <li>Authenticity,</li>
  <li>The Infrastructure in this sense the Blockchain</li>
</ol>

So an NFT can be defined as a type of digital asset that represents ownership or proof of authenticity of a unique item or piece of content using blockchain technology. Unlike cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin or Ethereum, which are fungible and can be exchanged on a one-to-one basis, NFTs are unique and cannot be exchanged on a like-for-like basis.

### The token standard for NFTs on Ethereum is ERC 721:

For those in the call who arent aware of Token standards, these are the set of rules that run crypto tokens on the blockchain. Look at them as free, open standards that describe how to build non-fungible or unique tokens on the Ethereum blockchain.

ERC in this case refers to Ethereum Request for Comment (ERC): This is essentially a set of technical documents containing guidelines on developing a smart contract.

Apart from ERC 721 which is the Token Standard for NFTs (Non Fungible Tokens) we have the ERC 20 which is the Token Standard for Fungible Tokens, ERC-777 which aims to address the limitations of ERC-20. This token standard makes it more efficient for smart contracts to send and receive tokens through a mechanism known as Hooks.

### Key Elements of NFTs

<ol>
  <li>
    Unique and Indivisible: Each NFT is distinct and cannot be replicated. Look at NFTs as
    indivisible, meaning it cannot be divided into smaller units like traditional cryptocurrencies,
  </li>
  <li>
    Blockchain Technology: NFTs are typically built on blockchain platforms, such as Celo, Arbitrum,
    and Ethereum being one of the most popular choices. Why are they built on chain if you ask: The
    blockchain ensures transparency, security, and immutability, providing a secure and verifiable
    record of ownership,
  </li>
  <li>
    Ownership and Authenticity: NFTs are used to represent ownership of digital or physical assets,
    such as digital art, music, videos, virtual real estate, in-game items, and more. They serve as
    a digital certificate of authenticity and provenance,
  </li>
  <li>
    Smart Contracts: NFTs often utilize smart contracts, which are self-executing contracts with the
    terms of the agreement directly written into code. Smart contracts automate the process of
    transferring ownership and can include royalty mechanisms for creators.
  </li>
  <li>
    Digital Art and Collectibles: One of the most popular use cases for NFTs is in the world of
    digital art. You can tokenize your work, and buyers can own a unique, verifiable copy. This has
    led to a surge in digital art marketplaces.
  </li>
  <li>
    Gaming: NFTs can also represent the gaming industry to represent in-game assets, characters, and
    items. Players can buy, sell, and trade these assets both within and outside the gaming
    ecosystem.
  </li>
  <li>
    Virtual Real Estate: Some virtual worlds and metaverse platforms use NFTs to represent ownership
    of virtual real estate. Users can buy, sell, and trade virtual land in these digital
    environments.
  </li>
  <li>
    Music and Entertainment: Musicians and content creators can tokenize their work, allowing fans
    to own a piece of digital content. This can include music, videos, or other digital media.
  </li>
  <li>
    Tokenization of Physical Assets: NFTs can also be used to represent ownership of physical
    assets. For example, a real estate property or a piece of rare physical art could have an
    associated NFT representing ownership.
  </li>
</ol>

### Key Considerations for Choosing a Blockchain for NFT Projects

### Scalability:

The blockchain Trillemma coined by Vitalik Buterin proposes a set of three main issues that developers encounter when building blockchains — that is decentralization, security and scalability — Scalability is a crucial factor to look at when selecting a Chain for your NFT projects the Chain should be able to support an enormous number of transactions and users without faltering by increasing fees and transaction times especially if you expect high transaction volumes. Assess the blockchain’s capacity to handle a large number of transactions per second (TPS) without compromising performance.

  <li>Transaction Throughput,</li>
  <li>Gas Fees,</li>
</ol>

### Security

Security is paramount when dealing with digital assets. Evaluate the blockchains security features, consensus mechanism, and track record for handling security issues. Consider blockchains with a proven history of security.

### Lets break them down one by one:

<ol>
  <li>
    Consensus Mechanism: We have different types of consensus mechanisms used by the blockchain,
    these are very crucial for the security of the specific and particular blockchain. On a high
    level we have the Proof-of-work (PoW) and proof-of-stake (PoS) which are the most common
    mechanisms but we also have other consensus mechanisms. I will leave you to do your own Research
    and let me know in the comments section, each of these mechanisms have their own security
    considerations. PoW relies on computational power, while PoS relies on staking tokens. You need
    to ensure the chosen mechanism aligns with the security requirements of your NFT project.,
  </li>
  <li>
    Smart Contract Security: NFTs often involve the use of smart contracts. Ensure the blockchain
    network that you choose has a secure smart contract environment. Consider platforms with robust
    security audits and mechanisms in place to minimize the risk of smart contract vulnerabilities.,
  </li>
  <li>
    Immutability: Immutability is one of the key features of blockchain technology. The immutability
    of a Chain ensures that once data is recorded, it cannot be altered. This is crucial for the
    integrity of NFT ownership records. You need to check how resistant the blockchain is to
    tampering and whether it has mechanisms in place to maintain data integrity.
  </li>
  <li>
    Interoperability: Talking of Interoperability, Interoperability refers to the ability of
    different Chains to communicate and share information, data and assets seamlessly. When
    selecting the Chain for your NFT projects you need to Consider the interoperability of the
    blockchain with other platforms and services. This can be important for the integration of your
    NFTs with other applications or marketplaces, and allows for the flexibility if you want to use
    your NFTs in various applications or platforms that operate on different blockchains.
  </li>
  <li>
    Community and Ecosystem Support: Another factor to consider when choosing the right blockchain
    for your NFT projects is the backing and strength of the Community and the overall ecosystem
    surrounding the chain.
  </li>
  <li>
    Environmental Sustainability: Environmental sustainability has become an increasingly important
    factor when building for the Blockchain, and NFT (Non-Fungible Token) projects are not an
    exception. The environmental impact of blockchain, particularly in the context of energy
    consumption and carbon footprint, has raised concerns, especially with the popularity and growth
    of NFTs.
  </li>
</ol>
